Polarizability prescription

A number of expressions for the polarizability are known [2]. ADDA implements the following: the Clausius–Mossotti (CM), the radiative reaction correction (RR, [45]), formulation by Lakhtakia (LAK, [46]), digitized Green’s function (DGF, [46]), approximate integration of Green’s tensor over the dipole (IGTSO, [47]), the lattice dispersion relation (LDR, [11]), corrected LDR (CLDR, [48]), and the Filtered Coupled Dipoles (FCD). The choice of the polarizability prescription is performed by command line option

-pol <type> [<arg>]

where <type> is one of the cldr, cm, dgf, fcd, igt_so, lak, ldr, nloc, nloc_av, rrc, so. <arg> is optional flag that can be only avgpol and only for LDR specifying that the LDR polarizability should be averaged over incident polarizations. Default is LDR without averaging. It is important to note that this is not the best option for all cases. Our experience shows that LDR may perform particularly badly (as compared to CM or RR) for very large refractive indices, and FCD (together with its interaction term, §10.2) becomes the best option [23].

Finally, other DDA improvements are known, which modify the polarizability (or permittivity) of the dipoles near the boundary. These improvements include weighted discretization [50] and spectral filtering of the permittivity that was proposed in combination with the FCD [19]. These ideas have not yet been implemented in ADDA.[45] However, adding new options is straightforward for anyone who is familiar with C programming language.[46]
