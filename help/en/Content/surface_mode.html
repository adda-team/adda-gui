Surface Mode

Apart from the default free-space mode, ADDA is applicable to particles (finite systems of scatterers) located above the plane surface parallel to the xy‑plane [37]. This is enabled by command line option:

-surf <h> {<mre> <mim>|inf}

where h is the distance from surface to the particle center (in μm). Following argument(s) specify the refractive index of the substrate (below the surface), assuming that the vacuum is above the surface. It is done either by two values (real and imaginary parts of the complex value) or as effectively infinite inf which corresponds to perfectly reflective surface (§7). The case of particle fully submerged into the non-absorbing substrate can also be handled by rescaling all refractive indices (including the vacuum above the substrate) by ms (§4.2) and rotating the reference frame appropriately.

Details of the implementation are described in other sections of the manual. There are, however, a number of important caveats:

·Significant additional time for initialization is required (§5, §12.7);

·The surface mode is incompatible with radiation forces (§11.6), Gaussian incident beam (§9.2), WKB initial field for the iterative solver (§12.1), and rotation of particle (§8).

·DDA accuracy, including required discretization (§4.1), in the surface mode is poorly studied and may strongly depend on the refractive index and location of substrate.

·Definitions of common light-scattering quantities, like Mueller scattering matrices or cross sections, are somewhat ambiguous when beam is incoming from or scattered into the substrate. See corresponding sections for specific definitions employed by ADDA (§10.4, §11). It is even more ambiguous for absorbing substrate. Still, above-the-substrate quantities are well-defined in all cases.
