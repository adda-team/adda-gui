Parallel performance

ADDA is capable of running on a multiprocessor system, parallelizing a single DDA simulation. It uses MPI for communication routines. This feature can be used both to accelerate the computations and to circumvent the single-computer limit of the available memory, thus enabling simulations with a huge number of dipoles. We do not discuss the parallel efficiency of ADDA in detail, since it largely depends on the hardware. However, in our experience it was usually larger than 70–90% on a modern computer clusters (if the FFT grid is optimally partitioned among the processors, §6.6), and close to 100% in sparse mode (§12.3). It is also important to note that the MPI interface may occupy significant amount of RAM on each node, thereby decreasing the RAM available for ADDA itself (see also §5 and §6.6).
