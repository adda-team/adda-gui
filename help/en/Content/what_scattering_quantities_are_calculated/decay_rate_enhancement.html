Decay rate enhancement

These quantities are calculated if and only if the point-dipole incident field is used (§9.2). A dipole with polarization p0 at position r0 has a certain decay rate in free-space, which is enhanced in the presence of nanoparticle. The emitted energy is, in general, partly absorbed by the particle (nonradiative part) and partly scattered to the infinity (radiative part). The relevant formulae are given in [65,66]. The part absorbed by the particle (nonradiative, NR) is given by Cabs (§11.4), while radiative part Crad = Сtot – Cabs, which is not equal to Csca since incident dipole field interferes with the scattered field. In other words, Crad is the scattering cross section of the whole set of dipoles, including both exciting one and those from discretization of the particle. (61). ADDA still calculates the enhancement factors using Eq. (65), i.e. normalizing to C0, but also saves Csurf/C0 into file CrossSec (§C.7), so ratios with respect to Csurf can be easily obtained manually. The important limitation exists for absorbing substrate, since Cabs does not include the absorption in the substrate. Then the total enhancement factor is correct, but its division into radiative and not radiative parts is not. The possible (complicated) workaround is to integrate the total far-field (sum of exciting and scattered fields) over the upper hemisphere.[61]
