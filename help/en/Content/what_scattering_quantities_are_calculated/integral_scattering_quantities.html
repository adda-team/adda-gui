Integral scattering quantities

All scattering quantities described in this section are saved to several files (§C.7), corresponding to each of two incident polarizations (CrossSec‑X and CrossSec‑Y) and to orientation-averaged results (CrossSec). For the latter the result is calculated for unpolarized incident light. However, it is equal to that for any incident polarization, if the full range of Euler angle α is used for averaging (§8.2). ADDA always calculates Cext and Cabs (together with corresponding efficiencies Qext, Qabs).[57] Optionally, it can calculate scattering cross section Csca (and efficiency Qsca) and normalized and non-normalized asymmetry vectors – g and gCsca respectively. Csca and g are defined by Eqs. (47) and (48) respectively, the z‑component of g is the usual asymmetry parameter <cosθ>. Values of cross sections are in units of µm2. All the efficiencies are calculated by dividing the corresponding cross section over the area of the geometrical cross section of the sphere with volume equal to that of dipole representation of the particle, .[58] Optional calculation of Csca, gCsca and g can be enabled, respectively, by command line options

-Csca

-vec

-asym

If g is calculated Csca and gCsca are also calculated automatically.[59] The calculation of g and Csca is performed by integration over the whole solid angle, using a grid of scattering angles. The grid is specified by default in file alldir_params.dat (see §B.3 for format) in a form suitable for the Romberg integration (§12.6). Integration points for θ are spaced uniformly in values of cosθ. Different grid file can be specified through the command line option

-alldir_inp <filename>

In most cases Csca can be accurately calculated as Cext − Cabs, without using ‑Csca option. As discussed in §10.4 accuracy of this method can be improved when Csca << Cabs by using smaller ε (§12.1). Whether this is more computationally effective than using ‑Csca or not depends on the particular problem. For instance, Cext and Cabs may coincide in (almost) all digits saved to file for particles much smaller than the wavelength, leaving integration of scattered fields over the solid angle as the only viable option. However, in this case the integration can be done analytically (in the free-space mode), using formulae for the Rayleigh regime. For a general non-symmetric particle formulae are cumbersome. When absorbing substrate is present (§7) the default definition of Csca is unphysical (§10.4). A more adequate approach may be to integrate the scattered field only in the upper hemisphere, which can be done by changing the range of θ to [0°,90°] in alldir_params.dat (§B.3).

Currently ADDA provides no built-in functionality for automatically scanning over a wavelength spectrum. However, a simple workaround is to execute ADDA for each wavelength independently, using a script, and then collect the output data.[60]
